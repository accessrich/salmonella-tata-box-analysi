           - Critical for initial infection

EXPECTED CONSEQUENCES:

BIOLOGICAL SIGNIFICANCE
================================================================================

SALMONELLA VIRULENCE FACTORS:

1. INVASION: SPI-1 encoded T3SS allows bacteria to cross intestinal epithelium
   - Target host cell mechanisms
   - Induce membrane ruffling and endocytosis
   - Enter non-phagocytic cells

2. SURVIVAL: SPI-2 encoded systems permit intracellular survival
   - Replicate within macrophage vacuoles
   - Evade phagolysosomal killing
   - Persist systemically

3. MOTILITY: Flagella enhance:
   - Chemotaxis toward infection sites
   - Penetration of mucus layers
   - Invasion of epithelial surfaces

4. IMMUNE EVASION: Phase variation allows:
   - Antigenic switching (O and H antigens)
   - Escape from antibody-mediated immunity
   - Long-term persistence
